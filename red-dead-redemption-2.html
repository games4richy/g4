Deep Dive into Overlooked Gameplay Mechanics

Red Dead Redemption 2 is renowned for its intricate details and often overlooked gameplay mechanics. Mastering these can greatly enhance the player's experience and immersion.

Weapon Familiarity

The more Arthur uses a particular weapon, the more proficient he becomes with it. Increased familiarity improves a gun's accuracy, range, and reload speed.

Modify Arthur's Weight

Arthur's body weight can be modified, affecting his abilities. A lighter Arthur is nimbler and faster at sprinting, while a heavier, bulkier Arthur will absorb the most bullet damage.

Quick Reload for Bolt Action Rifle

There is a far speedier way to reload the bolt action rifle: hit R2 immediately after firing and Arthur will lower the gun and restuff a bullet in there in the blink of an eye.

Switch Shoulders While Aiming

A simple tap of the left or right buttons on the controller D-pad whilst aiming which switch up the shoulder view. This technique is super useful in those close quarter gunfights when Arthur is routinely taking cover.

Spin Handgun into Holster

Arthur can spin his handgun back into its holster. There're two speeds to this too, quick, and slow. The slow spin gives the best opportunity to see the gun twirling into its holster.

Temporary Horse Follow

To convince another horse follow Arthur and his main is to hit bonding level one with it; pat, brush, feed, or whack Arthur’s saddle on and off for a fast track to bonding level one, and that other horse will follow Arthur and his main around indefinitely.

Disarming Enemies

By targeting NPC arms or hands, players force them to drop their weapons, leaving them vulnerable to attack. This is particularly useful during bounties, where players want to capture and bring them in alive.

Dodging While Aiming

Dodging while aiming is a crucial skill. By mastering this technique, players can avoid enemy attacks while maintaining accuracy.

Kicking Enemies While on Horseback

One of the most satisfying aspects is the ability to kick while on horseback. This quick and efficient attack can be a lifesaver in horseback combat, especially when players are outnumbered or need to take out a threat quickly.
